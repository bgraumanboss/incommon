InCommon

The Problem: College students lack connection to their collegiate communities. Specifically they miss casual interactions and informal conversations with professors. This loss is leading to a general deterioration of mental health among the student body. Our Solution: A Web application that facilitates organization of virtual communications, such a chats and video calls, while encouraging frequent and informal interactions.

Created by: Aine, Bea and Sara

Persona: George Forrest

George is a professor who is super passionate about teaching. Unfortunately, George struggles to connect with his students when classes are online. He is frustrated with the rigidity of appointment only virtual office hours and wishes there were some way to restore the community of his classroom even though everyone is far apart.

Allison just finished her first year of college. Before everything went remote, she bonded with her first year writing professor over their shared love of baking. IDEATION

SCENARIOS

Each Scenario was created to represent a task that one of our personas would need to complete.

Scenario One


Task: Schedule a meeting
Persona: Allison

Allison befriended her first year writing professor due to their shared love of baking. She just found a super old cookbook at a thrift store with a ton of vintage baking recipes and wants to share. The professor hosts a baking chatroom on our platform that Allison is a member of. Allison posts pictures of the cookbook in the chat so all members of the chatroom can see it. Additionally she sends out a video call request to the professor saying, “Hi professor! I just found this super old cookbook and was wondering if you’d like to see it”. She knew she was able to ask the professor to chat because they were online and it was during their “social hours”. Within minutes the professor accepts the request and they are video chatting. The professor opens up the call to the whole chat room so anyone who’s a member can come look at the cookbook.

Scenario Two


Task: Chat with a professor
Persona: Ann

Ann is a highly motivated and ambitious rising senior at Wellesley College. However, due to her fast paced and busy schedule, she’s found that she lacks the meaningful connections with professors that her peers seem to have made in their last few years. Ann largely attributes this loss to her inability to marry her hectic schedule of managing diving practices, academics, an off-campus internships, and two orgs with the office hours of any of her professors. In a context of online learning, Ann hoped originally that the forced reduction of her activities would allow her to at least make these mentorship connections with professors. However, this reality has not manifested. Zoom office hours with professors are solely for academic purposes. The presence of other students combined with professors’ preferences for recording their office hours leaves Ann with little time to connect personally with her professors. Ann also feels awkward about reaching out to her professors via email and sending a Zoom link since what she really wants is unstructured conversation and what her professors need in order to accept the invite is a concrete reason for meeting. Ann decides to chat with her professor initially before scheduling a zoom call in order to get to know her potential mentor in a low stakes environment. To do this, Ann navigates to the “people” tab on the chat page and searches for her professor’s name. She then writes her message in the chat box and presses send, keeping an eye on the active status of her professor in the “chat members” box. Ann could have started this chat from the directory by searching for her professor in the “people” tab and clicking on the link to chat but instead chose to start the chat from the chat page since she was already in the chem help room chatting with her SI and classmates about how to complete her problem set.

Scenario Three


Task: Join a video call
Persona: George

George, 37, is a professor in Wellesley’s Chemistry department. While on campus, he invests hours into connecting with students to answer questions, talk about trending TV shows, and give random life advice. Transitioning to remote learning seemed to exacerbate the challenges he faces in support students. He’s lost zoom links to meetings, emailed 10 times back and forth to schedule a 30 minute meeting, and is just about ready to call it quits. George would love to be more organized and available to his students but he doesn’t have the time to create an organized system on his own. He recently made an account on Our Interface and scheduled his first Zoom call with one of his advisees. In order to join the Zoom call, he opens up Our Interface on his laptop and first checks his personal appointments calendar. He spots his appointment with his advisee, Jen, and clicks on the description of the call. George is only a few minutes early, so he goes ahead and clicks on the link to join the zoom call, being sure to allow Zoom to take him to a new window when prompted. Jen joins a few minutes later and George is able to give her the advice she needs about choosing classes for the upcoming semester with the ease of an in person meeting.


DESIGN DIRECTION

Design One


Why we chose this design: creative/unique, visually appealing, intuitive design (high learnability and memorability due to signifiers)

Strengths: could translate well between desktop and mobile device; topics for messages; visually appealing, high satisfaction possible that could make it more well used and engaging

Weaknesses: would be difficult to make meeting/calendar section smaller, a bit overcomplicated; calendar could use more filters for different topics and types of contacts; not having weekend slots on calendar removes some of social focus

Design Two


Why we chose this design: high efficiency but learnability might be low since it’s so complex, memorability would be high since it looks like other things

Strengths: Very practical/efficient; very comprehensive since there are so many filters/options, lots of information, could hypothetically host the entire school; bandwidth; multifaceted nature (calendar, chat); directory is a good tool; pulls from a lot of existing tools at Wellesley but puts them together (directory, calendar, etc.); thorough

Weaknesses: Might be overwhelming at first; potential to be disorganized; potentially too much content; because it seems so practical it loses some of the social/creative aspect; harder to have casual conversations
